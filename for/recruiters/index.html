Why hiring signals beat generic firmographic lists

The standard recruiting BD playbook is: pull a firmographic list of "Series A+ B2B SaaS, 20–200 emp", spray openers about "hard-to-find engineers," wait. Reply rates have crashed to under 1% because every recruiter is doing the exact same thing. Signal-grounded outbound flips the equation:

  • You only reach companies who are actually hiring right now. A company that just closed a Series B and announced a "100 → 200 by year-end" target is buying recruiting services in the next 60 days regardless of who reaches out. Reaching them with proof you know that fact gets you in the door instead of in the spam folder.
  • The signal pre-qualifies fit. A senior engineering hire (e.g. new VP Eng from Stripe) tells you exactly what the next 10 hires will look like — which lets you mention candidates you already have in pocket without sounding generic.
  • Hiring managers respond to evidence, not enthusiasm. "Saw your VP Eng announce that Platform is the priority for Q3" is a much better opener than "I help fast-growing companies hire amazing engineers." One is intel; the other is noise.

The signal types we use for recruiting outbound

Signal Why it predicts a placement opportunity
Recent funding round Headcount plans typically follow a round by 30–90 days. Opener references round size + commonly correlated hiring focus (eng-heavy for infra/AI rounds; GTM-heavy for late-stage rounds).
Senior leader join (VP / C-level) New leadership rebuilds their team within 6 months. Opener names the new leader + their last team's shape as a tell of what they'll hire next.
New office / geographic expansion Means an entire local org to staff. Especially powerful for recruiters with deep local network in the new geo.
New product line / vertical launch New product = new team. Cite the launch + the typical role mix needed (e.g. "new fintech vertical → first compliance hire usually within 90 days").
Active job postings (specific shape) Cite the actual open req, mention the difficulty (e.g. "rare combo of payment-rails experience + leadership") + your specific angle.
Public hiring goal "Going from 60 → 200 by year-end" tweets, conference talks, blog posts. The most explicit signal — they're telegraphing the spend.
