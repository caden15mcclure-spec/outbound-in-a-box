Why this works better for SaaS founders specifically

B2B SaaS targets are unusually well-documented in public sources — funding announcements, Crunchbase, ProductHunt launches, GitHub release activity, hiring posts, LinkedIn product launches. That makes signal-grounded outbound disproportionately effective in this vertical:

  • The signals are dense and recent. A Series A round closes; a CTO joins; a major feature ships — all of it surfaces within days, all of it cite-able.
  • Your buyers respect work. Other technical founders / VPs recognize the difference between a templated email and one that proves you read their changelog. They reply at higher rates because it signals you're not just spraying.
  • The verticals are small. If your TAM is "AI infra Series A+ in US/CA/UK", that's maybe 300 companies total. Burning them with mail-merge spam means you have nothing left. Burning them with signal-grounded outreach gives you 300 actual conversations.

The signal types we use for SaaS

Signal What it tells the recipient
Recent funding round You know they just raised — so you know they're hiring, scaling outbound, and have budget. The opener references the round size + lead investor.
Senior hire (CTO / VP Eng / Head of Sales) New leadership = new priorities. The opener mentions the hire by name and ties their background to a likely upcoming initiative.
Product launch / shipped feature Cites a specific changelog entry, ProductHunt launch, or release note. Proves you actually use or follow their product.
Customer wins / case studies References a specific named customer they just landed. Especially powerful for prospects in adjacent verticals.
Open-source / dev activity References commit activity on a public repo or a tech blog post — for technical buyers (CTOs, VP Eng), this is the highest-trust signal.
Conference / podcast appearance "Caught your talk at [conf]…" — works only if the prospect actually gave the talk. We verify before citing.
