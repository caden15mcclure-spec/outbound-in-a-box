Why this works better for agencies than databases-plus-templates

Agencies tend to sell to clients who themselves have a marketing or product story they're proud of. That cuts both ways: easy to find a real signal, but the prospect is hyper-attuned to whether you actually read it or just grepped their LinkedIn for keywords.

  • Recent case studies are the single best signal. If a prospect just published a case study about cutting CAC by 40%, an opener that references the specific work + ties it to a gap in your service is the highest-conversion move you can make. We surface these per-prospect.
  • You're competing with other agencies, not with tool-sellers. The bar isn't "did you mention my company name" — it's "did you do work I couldn't have done for myself." Signal-grounded openers clear that bar.
  • Your tier rotation is small. A solo agency probably has a TAM of 500–2000 right-fit prospects total. You can't afford to spam them — but a 50-prospect signal-grounded batch every two weeks puts you at the top of inbox without exhausting the list.

The signal types we use for agency outbound

Signal Why it works for agency pitches
Recent client win / new logo Prospect just landed a marquee customer. Opener references it + ties to a likely follow-on need (e.g. expanded inbound, more content, brand refresh).
Published case study The case study itself reveals what the prospect cares about + what they think is working. Highest-trust signal for agency-to-agency-style pitches.
Rebrand / website refresh Means they're investing in marketing identity right now. Useful for design/brand/content agencies pitching adjacent services.
New service / vertical announcement Prospect just expanded their offering — there's likely a GTM gap as they figure out the new motion. Cite the announcement + offer the gap.
Hire of a marketing/growth lead New leadership tends to bring agency budget with them. Cite the hire by name + their last 1–2 employers as a tell of what they'll prioritize.
Conference talk / podcast appearance Cite specifically — proves you watched/listened. Works especially well if the topic overlaps with your agency's specialty.
